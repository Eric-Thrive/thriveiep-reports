The product problem

Important assessment results do not arrive in one clean format

A report may mix structured tables, narrative findings, figures, and repeated administrations. Each carries different evidence and context.

Tables

Dense, structured rows may hold a standard score, percentile, classification, and confidence interval together.

Narrative prose

Clinically important results are often written into paragraphs, especially for behavioral and executive-function measures.

Figures

Charts can be noticed and cited for review without turning geometry into an inferred score.

The core data model

V3 stores source facts and decisions in separate fields

A JSON object is a machine-readable list of named fields. The Redactor creates structured JSON, removes identifying information, and records what the source said and where it appeared. The BC report generator adds identity, policy, destination, review, and accounting.

Why separate preparation from report decisions?

The Redactor creates structured, de-identified evidence and prepares the handoff. It does not decide which assessment rule applies or whether a result belongs in Section 5. Those decisions stay with the BC report generator.

Metric-based inclusion

Inclusion is an explicit policy decision for each metric

The existing AE assessment bank is the single policy authority. It connects a recognized instrument and subscale to permitted metrics, direction of concern, classification authority, and Section 5 placement.

01 · SourceWhat the report saysExample“Working Memory Index · 92”
02 · IdentityWhich test and measureExampleWISC-V · Working Memory Index
03 · MetricWhich score rule appliesExampleStandard score · mean 100, SD 15
04 · MeaningWhether the value qualifiesExample92 is 8 points below the mean, so it qualifies under the planned 7-point rule
05 · DispositionWhat happens in the reportExampleInclude in General Cognitive Results
Cognitive and performance metrics

The 7/15-SD reporting boundary means 93 or lower on a common mean-100, SD-15 scale. This is broader than the separate 2/3-SD core/proof benchmark.

Behavioral rating scales

Direction-aware policy follows the instrument’s publisher-defined clinical tier or governed instrument-specific cutoff. Parent and teacher administrations stay separate.

Unfamiliar or incomplete tests

If the test or scale is unknown, V3 keeps the score and source location but withholds the result. It asks: “Which test and scale is this?” The answer selects the right rule.

Human review where it matters

When a result is unclear, V3 shows what needs review

Most results can continue through the process. When a detail is missing or two sources disagree, V3 keeps the evidence, identifies the problem, and sends it to a reviewer.

Where it came fromtable, paragraph, figure, or more than one place
What was founda usable value or an extraction problem
What it belongs tothe test, scale, date, and rater
What it meansa clear reporting rule or a question for review
What happens nextshow it, review it, withhold it, or avoid counting it twice
Examples of questions a reviewer may see
  • Which test date and rater does this score belong to?
  • Does this printed label apply to this exact scale?
  • Are the table and prose values the same result or a real conflict?
