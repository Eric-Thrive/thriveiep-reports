Critical Thinking

You may feel like nothing you do with thinking actually changes anything.

What this skill is

Critical thinking is how your brain evaluates information, spots patterns, and decides what matters. It's the engine behind your ability to learn from experience.

What's already strong
You ask good questions
When something doesn't add up, you notice. Your brain flags inconsistencies that others miss.
This shows up when you're evaluating new information.
You see multiple angles
You naturally consider different perspectives before forming an opinion. That's a real strength in college.
This comes through when you're working through group decisions.
You catch logical gaps
You notice when an argument doesn't hold together. That sharpens your thinking and your writing.
This shows up when you're reviewing your own work or someone else's.
Where you're growing

You sometimes get stuck analyzing when it's time to decide. The pause is valuable, but learning when to move forward is the next edge.
