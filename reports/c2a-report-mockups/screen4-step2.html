Where to focus next

Critical thinking isn't about being smarter. It's about using what you already have more intentionally.

This is:
building a habit of pausing before reacting
This is not:
overthinking everything or second-guessing yourself
Your next step

Start by noticing one moment each day where you could pause and ask "what am I assuming here?"

Simple things you can try

These are low-effort shifts that build critical thinking over time.

The 5-Second Pause
Before responding to a question or making a decision, count to five silently. Use that time to ask yourself what you might be missing.
Why: This builds the pause habit that's the foundation of critical thinking.
The Devil's Advocate Game
Pick any opinion you hold and spend 2 minutes arguing the opposite side, even if you disagree.
Why: This strengthens your ability to see multiple perspectives.
Ask for what you need
When someone puts you on the spot in class or a meeting
"I need a minute to think about this before I answer. Can we come back to it?"
When instructions are unclear and you need more clarity
"I want to make sure I understand. Can you say that a different way?"
