Why this matters for you

Critical thinking shapes how you learn, not just what you learn.

When you practice evaluating information and spotting assumptions, you build a foundation that transfers to every class, every conversation, and every decision. It's not about being right. It's about being thoughtful.

"This is a skill that compounds over time."

Patterns you might recognize
Asks clarifying questions Spots assumptions Connects ideas across topics Prefers evidence over opinion
What you might notice
In class
You may notice you're the one asking "but what about..." questions when the rest of the room has moved on.
With friends
You might catch yourself fact-checking things others share casually. That's your critical thinking showing up.

"Your thinking is your superpower. Trust it."
