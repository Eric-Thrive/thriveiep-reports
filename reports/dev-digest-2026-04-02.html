Terminal Agents Suffice for Enterprise Automation arXiv 68 upvotes on HuggingFace

Bechard et al. argue that coding agents equipped with only a terminal and filesystem outperform MCP-augmented and web-GUI agents on enterprise automation tasks, questioning whether complex agentic infrastructure is necessary given its cost and overhead.

Relevant: We rely heavily on MCP servers (code-graph, neo4j-kg, pm-hub-kg). This paper challenges that architectural choice for certain task classes.

Embarrassingly Simple Self-Distillation Improves Code Generation arXiv Apple ML | GitHub: apple/ml-ssd

Zhang et al. (Apple) show that sampling solutions from a model and fine-tuning on them (no verifier, no teacher, no RL) improves Qwen3-30B from 42.4% to 55.3% pass@1 on LiveCodeBench v6. Gains concentrate on harder problems.

Think Anywhere in Code Generation arXiv GitHub: jiangxxxue/Think-Anywhere

Recent Academic Papers (3)
MDPBench: First Multilingual Document Parsing Benchmark (17 Languages) arXiv GitHub: Yuliang-Liu/MultimodalOCR

Li et al. introduce MDPBench -- 3,400 document images spanning 17 languages and diverse photographic conditions. Current SOTA parsers fail severely on non-English and photographed documents.

Relevant: The Redactor pipeline handles multilingual IEP documents. This benchmark could validate our GLM-OCR choice against VLM alternatives.

[12]
Hydra: Unifying Document Retrieval and Generation in a Single VLM arXiv

Georgiou presents a dual-head approach providing both ColBERT-style retrieval and autoregressive generation from a single VLM via a switchable LoRA adapter, with byte-identical generation fidelity vs the base model.

[13]
PixelPrune: 22--71% of Document Visual Tokens Are Duplicates arXiv

Wang et al. observe that most visual tokens in document images are pixel-exact duplicates. PixelPrune uses predictive-coding compression to eliminate them, reducing VLM compute for document understanding without quality loss.

AI in Education & Disability Services

IEPs in the Age of AI: Examining IEP Goals Written with and without ChatGPT ERIC Journal of Special Education Technology, 2026

Waterfield et al. find no statistically significant difference (p=0.67) in quality between AI-generated and teacher-written IEP goals. Teachers reported positive perceptions of AI for workload management. The study recommends continued exploration and training for GenAI in special education.

Relevant: Directly validates the use of AI in IEP-adjacent workflows. ThriveIEP's C2A assessment complements this finding -- we measure the skills, AI helps write the goals.
