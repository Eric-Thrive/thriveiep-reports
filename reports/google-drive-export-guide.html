Google Drive Export Guide

ThriveIEP · User Guide · April 2026

This guide shows you how to export completed assessment reports to Google Drive as editable Google Docs or PDFs. It covers first-time setup, the export workflow, troubleshooting, and administrator controls.

Who is this for?

Educators and administrators who use ThriveIEP report pages (K-12, Tutoring, or Post-Secondary) and want to save reports to Google Drive for sharing, archiving, or compliance.

What you'll learn

  • How to connect your organization's Google Drive (one-time setup)
  • How to export any report as a Google Doc or PDF
  • What to do when something goes wrong
  • How administrators can manage the connection
Section 2 of 7

Overview

The Send to Google button lives in the report sidebar, next to Print and Download. It exports completed assessment reports directly to your organization's Google Drive.

Two export formats are available: an editable Google Doc (great for collaboration) or a PDF stored in Drive (great for archiving and compliance).

Before anyone can export, the organization's Google Drive must be connected. This is a one-time setup that any team member can do. Once connected, everyone in the organization can export.

Connecting Google Drive

If Google Drive hasn't been connected for your organization yet, the sidebar button shows "Connect Google Drive" instead of "Send to Google." Here's how to set it up:

First-Time Setup

1 Open any completed report (K-12, Tutoring, or Post-Secondary)

2 Find the Connect Google Drive button in the sidebar

3 Click the button. You'll be redirected to Google's sign-in page.

4 Sign in with the Google account you want to use for your organization

5 Review the permissions and click Allow

6 You'll be redirected back to your report automatically

7 A success notification will confirm: "Google Drive connected!"

The button now shows "Send to Google" with a dropdown arrow. You're ready to export.

Good to Know

  • This is a one-time setup per organization. Once connected, all team members can export.
  • ThriveIEP only requests permission to create files in Google Drive. It cannot read or modify your existing files.
  • If Drive is already connected by another team member, you'll see "Send to Google" immediately.

If Connection Fails

If something goes wrong during sign-in, you'll be redirected back to your report page with a red notification explaining what happened. Common causes: declining the permission prompt, or a temporary Google outage. Click "Connect Google Drive" again to retry.

Exporting a Report

Once Google Drive is connected, exporting takes just a few clicks.

Step 1: Open the Report

Navigate to any completed assessment report:

  • K-12 reports
  • Tutoring reports
  • Post-Secondary reports

Step 2: Click "Send to Google"

Find the Send to Google button in the sidebar. Click it to open the export dropdown.

Step 3: Choose Export Format

Format What It Creates Best For
Google Doc DEFAULT An editable Google Doc in your Drive Collaborative editing, adding notes, sharing with team
PDF to Drive A PDF file uploaded to your Drive Archiving, attaching to student records, printing

Step 4: Wait for Export

  • The button shows a spinner with "Sending Doc..." or "Sending PDF..."
  • Export typically takes a few seconds
  • Do not navigate away while the export is in progress

Step 5: View in Google Drive

  • When the export completes, the document opens automatically in a new browser tab
  • A success notification confirms the file was created
  • The file is saved in your Google Drive and accessible from drive.google.com

Troubleshooting

If something isn't working, find your issue below.

Problem Cause Solution
Button shows "Connect Google Drive" Drive not yet connected for your org Click the button and complete the Google sign-in flow
Button shows "Checking Google..." Page is loading connection status Wait a moment. The button updates once the status check completes.
Export fails with "Drive not connected" The connection may have expired Ask an administrator to reconnect in Organization Settings
Export fails with "Drive connection expired" OAuth tokens expired An administrator needs to reconnect in Organization Settings
Nothing happens when clicking Connect Browser may be blocking the redirect Check for popup blockers; allow popups for this site
"Drive already connected" error A team member already connected Drive This is expected. You can proceed to export directly.
Export button disabled / spinner stuck A previous export may still be running Refresh the page and try again
Document doesn't open in new tab Browser blocked the popup Check your popup blocker. The file is still in your Google Drive.

For Administrators

Managing the Google Drive Connection

Administrators can manage the integration from Organization Settings:

1 Navigate to Admin → Organizations → your organization

2 Click the Integrations tab

3 From here you can:
• View the current connection status and connected email
• Disconnect Google Drive (stops all exports for the org)
• Reconnect with a different Google account

Who Can Connect?

Action Allowed Roles
Initial connection (when Drive is not yet connected) ALL ROLES Customer, Org Admin, System Admin
Disconnect or reconnect (when Drive is already active) ADMIN ONLY Org Admin, System Admin

This prevents accidental disruption of an existing, working connection.

Compliance Notes

  • All exports are logged server-side for FERPA audit compliance
  • Exported files go to the Google Drive of the account used during connection
  • Token data is encrypted at rest using AES-256-GCM
