Section 01 of 10

Executive Summary

Query. Two claims from a prior 137 paper brief on agent harnesses for document products were pressure tested: (6) SOP encoded prompts beat free form agent collaboration in regulated domains, and (7) minimal loops scale further than framework heavy orchestrators. We searched 8 APIs and reviewed 95 papers published mid 2025 through 21 April 2026.

Key Messages

  • Proceed with Bloom Report roadmap SP1 (Temporal), SP2 (citation verification), SP3 (learning evaluator) as scoped. Do not add SP4.
  • The evidence converges on one pattern: bounded agentic workflow. A fixed pipeline backbone with constrained agents inside individual stages, governed by a dedicated verifier or auditor with veto authority.
  • Two scope additions. Add an agent registry with version pinning to SP1 (agent sprawl mitigation). Recalibrate SP3 to improve reviewer confidence, not reduce review time (accountability paradox).

What did we test?

Whether a recent literature window (post 15 June 2025) would invalidate two load bearing design claims behind the Bloom Report harness roadmap. If either claim inverted, the plan was to add a fourth sub project (SP4) scoping a migration from SOP encoded to agentic orchestration. Otherwise, ship SP1 through SP3 as drafted.

What did we find?

Both claims hold. Direct benchmark evidence from Amazon Science's SOP Bench (1,800 plus regulated domain tasks) shows Function Calling agents at 27 percent and ReAct agents at 48 percent, whereas a fixed pipeline with encoded procedures trivially scores 100 percent on known tasks. Five federal instruments (HTI 1, HIPAA 2025 NPRM, Section 1557 Final Rule, Department of Education AI Priority, ONC DSI transparency) all push in the same direction: tech asset inventories, audit trails, and algorithm transparency. The NeurIPS 2025 MAST failure taxonomy documents 41 to 86.7 percent production failure rates across 1,600 plus traces of multi agent systems.

What does this mean for the Bloom Report roadmap?

Ship SP1 through SP3 as scoped. Do not replace the 10 element pipeline with a top level planner agent. Label the Category 1 discrepancy gate as exception based human in the loop (HITL). Expect hallucination rates spanning 1.47 to 61.6 percent (MEDAI LLM SUMM systematic review, 216 publications), and plan citation verification as a dedicated auditor role, not a side effect of the generator.

What remains uncertain?

No randomized trial directly compares SOP versus planner agent clinical report generation in production. Therapeutic placement report generation has zero direct literature. Minimum viable audit trail log schemas for bounded agent workflows are undocumented. These gaps do not block the roadmap but do define the next research window.

Section 03 of 10

Verdict on Claim 6: SOP Encoded Beats Free Form

Claim 6. SOP encoded prompts beat free form agent collaboration in regulated domains. HOLD

Direct benchmark evidence

SOP Bench (Amazon Science, June 2025)

Moderate 1,800 plus regulated domain tasks across aerospace, insurance, and manufacturing SOPs. Function Calling agents score 27 percent. ReAct agents score 48 percent. A fixed pipeline baseline with encoded SOPs trivially scores 100 percent on any task whose procedure is already known.

Free form agents fail the majority of tasks where the procedure is already documented. The open question is not whether SOPs work, but how to encode them.

Regulatory reinforcement (5 federal instruments, HIGH tier)

  • 45 CFR § 92.210 (Section 1557 Final Rule). Identify and mitigate duties on AI clinical decision support, no Software as a Medical Device safe harbor.
  • 89 FR 1192 (HTI 1). Decision Support Intervention transparency attributes at 45 CFR 170.315(b)(11).
  • 90 FR 898 (HIPAA NPRM 2025). Removes the required versus addressable distinction; mandates tech asset inventory plus network map.
  • 89 FR 37522 (Section 1557 Final Rule commentary). AI nondiscrimination requirements.
  • 91 FR 18774 (ED Priority on AI). Effective 13 May 2026.

Clinical convergence

Frontiers in AI ORCH 2026 (deterministic orchestration beats stochastic). npj Digital Medicine 2025 clinical safety framework. Information Fusion 2025 AI Agent versus Agentic AI taxonomy, which formalizes the distinction the roadmap depends on.

Verdict on Claim 7: Minimal Loops Scale Further

Claim 7. Minimal loops scale further than framework heavy orchestrators. HOLD

Production failure evidence

  • Gartner (June 2025, LOW practitioner). Over 40 percent of agentic AI projects cancelled by end 2027.
  • MAST Failure Taxonomy (NeurIPS 2025, MODERATE). 14 distinct failure modes across 1,600 plus production traces. 41 to 86.7 percent production failure rates. Top failure classes: specification ambiguity and verification gaps.
  • LangChain framework reckoning telemetry (LOW practitioner). 45 percent of framework deployments never shipped; 23 percent removed after shipping.

Architectural direction from authoritative vendors

  • Anthropic Building Effective Agents 2025. Claude cookbooks orchestrator workers pattern. Recommends bounded workflow patterns over emergent planners.
  • Temporal plus OpenAI Agents SDK GA (23 March 2026). Durable execution framework with workflow and activity history. Adopted by OpenAI Codex and Replit production migrations.

No invert: SP4 is not needed

No paper in the window supports replacing a fixed clinical pipeline with a top level planner agent. Komodo Health's Marmot is the closest candidate in market and explicitly markets bounded, verifiable agents, not emergent planners.

Section 05 of 10

Key Findings

1. Bounded agency is a named paradigm Moderate

Four independent sources converge on the same pattern: Planner Auditor Twin (arXiv 2601.21113), Plan Execute Generate Judge (IEEE BigData 2025), Verifiable Workflow Automator (arXiv 2512.01453), and Zhang 2026.

A fixed pipeline backbone with constrained agents inside stages, governed by a dedicated verifier with veto authority.

2. HITL gate design is load bearing High

TAJET 2026 systematic review, npj Digital Medicine 2025, and MEDAI LLM SUMM 2026 (216 publications) converge. Exception based HITL is the only scalable class. Hallucination rates span 1.47 to 61.6 percent.

3. Hallucination is pipeline level, not model level High

npj Digital Medicine, JAMIA LLM as Judge, Nature Communications SourceCheckup. 50 to 90 percent of LLM citations are unsupportive of the claim they accompany. Citation verification must be a designated auditor role.

4. Audit trail requirements drive architecture High

Regulatory (5 federal instruments) plus architectural (Agentic AI Governance arXiv 2601.15630, Temporal workflow history, HIPAA 2025 NPRM). Control plane audit logging is the named governance pattern.

5. Agent Sprawl as operational risk Moderate

Governance and Lifecycle paper (arXiv 2601.15630, 2026). Undocumented, duplicated, or version drifted agents accumulate faster than organizations document them. Mitigation: centralized agent registry with version pinning.

6. IEP and educational generation: rich SOP evidence, thin agentic evidence High

Rakap 2024 RCT (n=22, Exceptional Children) plus Waterfield 2026 convergent mixed methods. Single call LLM is greater than or equal to human quality on IEP goals. EduPlanner (IEEE TLT 2025) remains MODERATE prototype with required teacher reflection loops.

7. Regulatory posture favors fixed pipeline High

Five federal instruments direct covered entities toward deterministic, auditable, inventoried AI systems. This is a structural, not aesthetic, preference.

Section 06 of 10

Consensus and Debate

Consensus (3 or more independent sources)

  • Bounded agency for regulated document generation. Four independent papers, converging terminology.
  • Exception based HITL as the only scalable class. TAJET 2026 plus npj Digital Medicine plus MEDAI LLM SUMM.
  • Source grounded verification required. SourceCheckup plus JAMIA LLM as Judge plus Agentic RAG Survey.
  • Temporal style durable execution for audit trails. OpenAI Agents SDK GA 2026, Replit and Codex production migrations, Anthropic orchestrator workers pattern.
  • Retrieval Augmented Generation as default for grounded clinical output. JAMIA OR 1.35 benefit, Agentic RAG Survey.

Debate (legitimate disagreement across sources)

  • Multi agent help on narrative tasks. MedAgentBoard 2025 disputes: single advanced LLM greater than multi agent on medical VQA, EHR, and QA. Planner Auditor Twin says two agent bounded designs still help.
  • HITL review time reducibility. Accountability Paradox literature says review time is invariant; MEDAI LLM SUMM 2026 finds conditions where it is reducible. Affects SP3 calibration target.
  • Minimum viable audit trail log schemas. Thin literature; the Control Plane Audit Logging pattern is named but not schema standardized.
Section 07 of 10

Gaps

Three gaps matter for the Bloom Report roadmap and are flagged for follow up research windows.

No direct RCT comparing SOP versus planner agent clinical report generation in production

The benchmark window (SOP Bench, MedAgentBoard) shows convergence but not head to head production comparison. Nearest adjacent evidence: ORCH Frontiers 2026 on deterministic versus stochastic orchestration; MAST on multi agent failure modes. Gap justifies the caution of holding SP4 out of scope rather than a positive case for planner agents.

Therapeutic placement report generation: zero direct literature

ERIC and Europe PMC searches returned no peer reviewed evidence on AI generation of therapeutic placement recommendations. Adjacent: IEP goal generation (Rakap, Waterfield), medical discharge summary generation (MEDAI LLM SUMM). Bloom Report treats this gap as known; placement logic stays deterministic for now.

FERPA plus LLM pipeline guardrails: zero peer reviewed ERIC coverage

No ERIC indexed paper addresses the intersection. HIPAA 2025 NPRM analogues apply structurally but not legally. Follow up research window needed before any Bloom Report FERPA covered deployment.

Minimum viable audit trail log schema for bounded agent workflows: undocumented

The Control Plane Audit Logging concept is named (Agentic AI Governance arXiv 2601.15630) but no reference schema maps 45 CFR § 164.312(b) audit controls to bounded agent tool call traces. Temporal's workflow history is the closest de facto standard. Priority follow up for SP1 implementation.

Section 09 of 10

What This Means for Us

Seven recommendations tied directly to Bloom Report roadmap decisions, severity rated for sequencing.

Critical Proceed with SP1, SP2, and SP3 as scoped

Action. Ship SP1 (Temporal durable execution), SP2 (citation verification), SP3 (learning evaluator) without architectural rework.

Evidence: 95 papers converge on bounded agentic workflow; 5 federal regulations demand the audit trail Temporal already provides; SOP Bench shows free form agents under 50 percent on regulated tasks.

Critical Do NOT add SP4 (SOP to agentic migration)

Action. Hold SP4 out of the roadmap. Revisit only if a production grade planner agent RCT in clinical settings publishes and inverts.

Evidence: no paper supports replacing fixed clinical pipelines with top level planner agents; MAST 2025 documents 41 to 86.7 percent failure rates in framework heavy systems.

High Add agent registry and version pinning to SP1 scope

Action. Every production agent gets a stable ID, owner, version, and sunset review date. Prompt, model version, and tool set pinned per agent version.

Evidence: Agent Sprawl identified as emerging operational risk in Governance and Lifecycle paper (arXiv 2601.15630, 2026). Temporal captures per call logs; missing layer is agent identity metadata.

High Label the Category 1 discrepancy gate as exception based HITL

Action. Rename and document the Category 1 gate using the exception based HITL framing in the roadmap spec.

Evidence: TAJET 2026 systematic review plus npj Digital Medicine clinical safety framework both identify exception based HITL as the only scalable HITL class. Aligns with existing Bloom Report stop the line triggers.

High Recalibrate SP3 objective to reviewer confidence, not review time

Action. Change SP3 success metric from review time reduction to reviewer confidence calibration (TRIPOD LLM output format).

Evidence: Accountability Paradox literature (reviewer time invariance) contra MEDAI LLM SUMM. Conservative posture wins on audit defensibility and regulatory alignment.

Moderate Add Verifiable Workflow Automator (VWA) terminology to the roadmap spec

Action. Update roadmap glossary and architecture prose to use VWA and bounded agentic workflow interchangeably with bounded pipeline.

Evidence: arXiv 2512.01453 names the pattern we are building. Shared vocabulary with the literature tightens future citation traceability.

Moderate Update SP2 rationale citing SourceCheckup

Action. Amend SP2 rationale section of the roadmap spec to cite the SourceCheckup finding.

Evidence: Nature Communications SourceCheckup documents 50 to 90 percent of LLM citations as unsupportive of their claims. Strengthens citation verification as a gating stage, not a best effort annotation.

Open questions for the next research window

  • Does any planner agent architecture match bounded workflow quality at equal HITL cost in clinical settings? (Currently no direct RCT.)
  • What is the minimum viable audit trail log schema that satisfies both 45 CFR § 164.312(b) and emerging AI governance framework requirements?
  • Does citation verification latency erode reviewer trust enough to force a fast path bypass, even when verification is load bearing?
